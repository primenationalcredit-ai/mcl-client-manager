# McCarthy Law (MCL) Client Manager — Complete Training Guide

## Part 1: Understanding the Program

### What Is an FCRA Violation?

FCRA stands for the Fair Credit Reporting Act. It is a law that says credit bureaus and creditors must report information correctly on your credit report.

An FCRA violation happens when something on a client's credit report is wrong. Here are some examples of violations:

- An account shows a balance on one credit bureau but shows as paid on another
- The same account shows up twice (duplicate)
- A creditor reports a balance after the client already paid or settled
- Information that was supposed to be removed is still showing
- A creditor did not properly investigate a dispute

These are not small mistakes. These are legal violations. When proven, the client could receive between $1,000 and $2,500 in compensation.

### What Does McCarthy Law Do?

McCarthy Law is a law firm that reviews our clients' credit reports looking for these mistakes. When they find a real FCRA violation, they can take legal action against the creditor on the client's behalf.

Here is how it works:

1. McCarthy Law reviews the credit report
2. They identify the specific error and which creditor is responsible
3. They let us know the client's name and what the error is
4. We call the client and explain what was found
5. If the client agrees, McCarthy Law handles the legal process
6. The client does not pay anything — McCarthy Law works on a contingency basis

### Why ASAP Credit Repair Offers This

We offer this service because:

- It fixes problems that normal disputes cannot resolve
- It gives clients extra protection when errors are serious
- Clients may receive financial compensation ($1,000-$2,500)
- It builds trust by offering more than just basic credit repair
- It sets us apart from companies that only do standard disputes

### Important Rule: Do Not Dispute Accounts Under Litigation

Once a client is accepted for McCarthy Law litigation, that account must NOT be disputed through our normal process. The account is frozen. No letters. No automation. No manual disputes. This is non-negotiable. Manager approval is required to change this status.

## Part 3: The Pipeline — How a Client Moves Through the System

Every client follows a path. Not every client makes it to the end. Some will stop responding and be moved to Lost. That is normal.

Here is the journey:

**Step 1 → Need to Call**
McCarthy Law found a violation on the client's credit report. We need to call the client and explain what was found.

**Step 2a → LVM (Left Voicemail)**
We called but the client did not answer. We left a voicemail. Now we follow up with emails and texts to get them to respond.

**Step 2b → Contacted**
We called and spoke to the client. They know about the violation. We send them the McCarthy Law intro email.

**Step 3 → Waiting on MCL**
The client responded "Yes" to the McCarthy Law email. We are now waiting for McCarthy Law to review their case and send a dispute packet.

**Step 4 → Packet Sent**

**Step 5 → Disputes Mailed**

**Step 6 → Waiting on Reports**
The credit bureau response deadline (the "Due Date") has passed. We need the client to pull their updated credit reports from annualcreditreport.com and send them to us. The app automatically moves clients here when the due date passes.

**Step 7 → Litigation**
The credit report error was NOT fixed after disputes. McCarthy Law is now suing the creditor. This is a win — the client may receive $1,000-$2,500 in compensation.

**Lost**
Client did not respond after all follow-up attempts, was not eligible, declined the offer, or the case was resolved without needing litigation.

**Do Not Call (Temporary)**
These are clients who have FCRA violations but are currently in a stage where we cannot help them right now. For example, they may be in the middle of active credit repair services, or we need to wait for a round to complete. This is a temporary hold — not a permanent status. These clients will eventually be moved back into the active pipeline. Clients in Do Not Call never show as overdue and have no follow-up reminders.

## Part 6: Working the Need to Call Pipeline

These are clients where McCarthy Law found a violation but we have not talked to them yet. This is always your first priority.

**Step by step:**

1. Click "Need to Call" in the left sidebar
2. Click on the first client in the list. Start with overdue clients (red highlights) if there are any.
3. Look at their profile. Check if they have a phone number. If not, look it up and add it.
4. Review the "Credit Report Error" section so you know what the violation is
5. Call the client. Explain that we found a possible legal violation on their credit report and that McCarthy Law can help at no cost to them.
6. After the call, you will see a green bar on their profile that says "Called this client?" Click one of the three buttons:
   - **Spoke to Client** — They answered. The client moves to Contacted.
   - **Left Voicemail** — They did not answer but you left a message. The client moves to LVM.
   - **No answer** — Nobody picked up and you did not leave a message. The client stays in Need to Call.
7. If the client does not have an email or phone on file, the app will ask you to enter them before moving the client. You MUST enter a valid email (must contain @) and phone number (10 digits) to continue.
8. Add a call note in the Communication Log. Select your name from the dropdown, type what happened, and click "+ Add." Even a short note like "LVM" or "Spoke - will reply to email" is helpful.
9. Move on to the next client.

**Always add a call note.** This helps the whole team know what happened without having to ask you.

This is one of the most important parts of the process. McCarthy Law depends on us to keep them updated on every client. If they do not have the latest information, they cannot do their job and the client's case stalls.

**The rule is simple: If something changes with a client, McCarthy Law needs to know about it.**

If a client sends you an email, forward it to fcra@mccarthylawyer.com right away. Do not wait. Do not summarize it later. Forward the actual email immediately.

**Option 2: Use the Email McCarthy Law button in the app.**

1. Open the client's profile

Here is a list of things that MUST be forwarded or communicated to McCarthy Law:

- **Any email a client sends us** — forward the email to fcra@mccarthylawyer.com
- **Any text message from a client** — use Email McCarthy Law in the app to relay what they said

## Part 12: Pausing and Scheduling Follow-ups

### Pausing Automations

Sometimes a client tells you they need a few days, or they are getting you some documents. You do not want the app marking them as overdue while they are working on it.

1. Open the client profile
2. Click the orange **Pause** button in the top right
3. A popup asks you for a reason. Type something like "Client getting documents" or "On vacation until 3/15"
4. Click OK

While paused:

- An orange banner shows on their profile with the reason you typed
- They will NOT show as overdue
- No follow-up actions will be suggested
- The pause is logged in the Communication Log with the date

To unpause: Click the green **Unpause** button. Everything goes back to normal and follow-ups resume.

### Scheduling a Follow-up Date

You can schedule a specific follow-up date for any client in any active pipeline (except Lost, Won, or Litigation). This is useful when a client says "call me next week" or "I will have the documents by Friday."

1. Open the client profile
2. Click the blue **Schedule** button in the top right
3. Enter a date (MM/DD/YYYY)
4. Click OK

What happens:

- A blue banner appears on the profile showing the scheduled date
- The client list shows the follow-up date under their name
- All automated follow-up actions are paused until that date — the app will NOT suggest sending emails or texts before then
- When the date arrives, the client turns RED and is counted in the overdue badge as a reminder to follow up
- Moving the client to another stage automatically clears the scheduled date

To clear a scheduled date early: Click the **Clear Date** button on the banner.

This is different from Pausing. Pausing is open-ended with no date. Scheduling means "do not follow up until THIS specific date, then remind me."

## Part 16: How Dates Move Clients Automatically

There are three important dates that drive the pipeline. These are updated by McCarthy Law and sync into the app automatically.

**Dispute Mailed to Client**
When McCarthy Law mails the dispute packet to the client, this date gets set. The app automatically moves the client to the Packet Sent pipeline. You can see this date on the client's profile under "Packet Mailed to Client."

**Client Confirmed Dispute Mailed**
When the client confirms they mailed the dispute packet to the credit bureaus, this date gets set. The app automatically moves the client to Disputes Mailed. You can see this on the profile under "Client Confirmed Mailed."

**Due Date (Round Over)**
This is when the credit bureau response period ends. When this date passes, the app automatically moves the client to Waiting on Reports. This is when we need to contact the client for their updated reports. The profile shows this date under "Due Date (Round Over)" with a countdown of how many days are left.

## Part 17: Communication Log Best Practices

The Communication Log is the single most important part of the client profile. It is the record of everything that happened.

**What gets logged automatically:**

- Every email sent (with the subject and message content)
- Every text message sent (with the message content)
- Every resend
- When automations are paused (with the reason) or unpaused
- When the client responded to McCarthy Law
- Stage changes

**What YOU need to log:**

- Every phone call (even if it is just "LVM" or "No answer")
- Important client responses
- Any special instructions or situations

**How to add a note:**

1. Select your name from the dropdown
2. Type your note in the text box
3. Click "+ Add" or press Enter

**Good notes look like this:**

- "LVM - left message about credit report error"
- "Spoke - client will reply to MCL email this week"
- "Client said they mailed packet yesterday"
- "Client on vacation, call back 3/20"

**Bad notes look like this:**

- "Called" (too vague — what happened?)
- No note at all (the worst — nobody knows what happened)

## Part 19: Do Not Call Pipeline

This is a temporary hold for clients who have FCRA violations but are in a stage where we cannot help them right now. For example, they may be in active credit repair services.

When you move a client to Do Not Call, the app requires two things:

1. **A reason** — why is this client being held? (e.g. "In active services" or "Client on payment plan")
2. **A follow-up date** — when should we check back? (e.g. 04/15/2026)

The client will sit in Do Not Call with no overdue alerts until that follow-up date arrives. On that date, the client turns RED as a reminder to move them back into the active pipeline.

When you move the client out of Do Not Call to another stage, the reason and follow-up date are automatically cleared.
